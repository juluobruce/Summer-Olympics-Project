Russia

Russia, also known as the Russian Federation, has competed at the modern Olympic Games on many occasions, but as different nations in its history. The Russian Empire first competed at the 1900 Games, and returned again in 1908 and 1912.

After the Russian revolution in 1917, and the subsequent establishment of the Soviet Union in 1922, it took 30 years until Russian athletes once again competed at the Olympics, as the Soviet Union at the 1952 Summer Olympics. After the dissolution of the Soviet Union in 1991, Russia competed as part of the Unified Team in 1992, and finally returned once again as Russia at the 1994 Winter Olympics. Due to the recent scandal in the 2016 Olympics, some athletes from Russai were ban from competing and go their medals taken away.

Russia has a total of 374 medals, 161 males & 213 females.
